# 🚀 7-Day Generative AI Tools Bootcamp
### From Zero to AI Power User — All Free Tier, All Hands-On

---

> **How to use this guide:** Each day covers 1–2 tools with a real-world scenario, step-by-step instructions, and expected outcomes. Every scenario runs entirely on free tiers. Set aside 2–3 hours per day.

---

## 📅 DAY 1 — NotebookLM: Your AI Research Assistant
**Theme:** Turn a mountain of reading into instant intelligence

---

### 🎯 Scenario: Researching a Business Idea

You are planning to start a small cloud kitchen business. You have found 3 useful resources online — a food industry article, a PDF guide on cloud kitchens, and a YouTube video on startup financing. Instead of spending hours reading everything, you will use NotebookLM to extract insights, generate a study guide, and create a podcast to listen to while commuting.

---

### 🔧 Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Part A — Setting Up NotebookLM

**Step 1: Create Your Account**
- Open your browser and go to **notebooklm.google.com**
- Sign in with your existing Google account (completely free, no credit card needed)
- You will land on the NotebookLM home page

**Step 2: Create a New Notebook**
- Click the **"+ New Notebook"** button on the top left
- Name it: `Cloud Kitchen Business Research`
- You will see three panels: Sources (left), Chat (centre), Studio (right)

---

#### Part B — Adding Sources

**Step 3: Add Your First Source — a PDF**
- Click **"+ Add"** in the Sources panel on the left
- Select **"Upload file"** from the dropdown
- Upload any PDF you have — for example, a government FSSAI food licensing document, or download any free food business PDF from the web
- Wait 30–60 seconds for NotebookLM to process it

**Step 4: Add a Web Article**
- Click **"+ Add"** again
- Select **"Website"**
- Paste this URL (or any freely accessible food business article): `https://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/food-truck.asp`
- Click **"Insert"**

**Step 5: Add a YouTube Video**
- Click **"+ Add"** again
- Select **"YouTube"**
- Paste the URL of any freely available YouTube video about cloud kitchen or food startup (search "cloud kitchen business model" on YouTube and copy the link)
- Click **"Insert"**

> ✅ You should now have 3 sources visible in the left panel

---

#### Part C — Asking Smart Questions

**Step 6: Chat with Your Sources**
- In the centre Chat panel, type the following questions one by one and observe the responses:

  - `"What are the key costs involved in starting a cloud kitchen?"`
  - `"What licences do I need to legally operate a food business in India?"`
  - `"Summarise the top 3 risks mentioned across all my sources"`
  - `"Create a SWOT analysis for a cloud kitchen startup based on these sources"`

- Notice how every answer has citation chips below it — click them to see which source the information came from

**Step 7: Ask a Comparison Question**
- Type: `"How does the advice in the article differ from the YouTube video on startup funding?"`
- This shows NotebookLM's ability to cross-reference multiple sources simultaneously

---

#### Part D — Generating Content from the Studio Panel

**Step 8: Generate an Audio Podcast**
- Look at the **Studio panel** on the right side
- Under "Audio Overview", click **"Generate"**
- NotebookLM will create a 5–8 minute podcast with two AI hosts discussing your research
- Once generated, click **Play** and listen
- Click the **interactive mode button** (microphone icon during playback) and interrupt the hosts by asking: `"Can you explain the licensing part in more detail?"`

**Step 9: Generate a Study Guide**
- In the Studio panel, look for **"Study Guide"** under Notes
- Click it — NotebookLM generates a structured summary with key concepts, facts, and a mini FAQ

**Step 10: Generate a Mind Map**
- In the Studio panel, click **"Mind Map"**
- You will see a visual tree diagram of all the concepts connected across your sources
- Explore the nodes by clicking on them

**Step 11: Create a Quiz**
- Click **"Quiz"** in the Studio panel
- Set difficulty to **Medium**, number of questions to **10**
- In the topic box, type: `"Focus on legal compliance and cost structures"`
- Click **"Generate"**
- Take the quiz to test your understanding

**Step 12: Generate a Slide Deck**
- Click **"Slide Deck"** in the Studio panel
- Wait 1–2 minutes
- A presentation is created, automatically opened in Google Slides
- Review and edit it as a ready-made pitch deck for your cloud kitchen idea

---

### 📊 Expected Outcome by End of Day 1
- You understand a complex topic without reading every source word-for-word
- You have a podcast, mind map, quiz, and slide deck — all from the same research session
- You understand how to cross-reference multiple document types with a single AI tool

## 📅 DAY 2 — Wispr Flow + Google Stitch
**Theme:** Speak your thoughts, design your vision

---

### 🎯 Scenario A (Wispr Flow): Writing Your Weekly Team Update Report Using Your Voice

You are a team lead. Every Friday you write a status report for your manager covering project progress, blockers, and next week's plan. Today, instead of typing it out, you will dictate the entire report using Wispr Flow while your hands remain free.

---

### 🔧 Wispr Flow — Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Setup

**Step 1: Download Wispr Flow**
- Go to **wisprflow.ai** in your browser
- Click **"Download for macOS"** (also available for Windows and iPhone)
- Install the app — it sits in your menu bar/system tray

**Step 2: Create a Free Account**
- Open the app after installation
- Sign in with Google or email
- The free tier gives you generous dictation minutes per month

**Step 3: Set the Trigger Shortcut**
- In Wispr Flow settings, note the trigger keyboard shortcut (usually `Double-press Fn` or a custom key)
- Keep this shortcut in mind — pressing it activates listening mode

---

#### Using Wispr Flow for Your Report

**Step 4: Open Google Docs**
- Go to **docs.google.com** and open a new blank document
- Title it: `Weekly Status Report — [Your Name] — Week of [Date]`

**Step 5: Dictate the Report**
- Click inside the document
- Press your Wispr Flow trigger shortcut
- You will see a small blue waveform indicator — you are now being recorded
- Speak naturally (do NOT pause awkwardly between words):

  *Say this:* "This week the team completed the user authentication module and integrated it with the payment gateway. We faced one blocker — the third-party SMS API kept timing out after 3 seconds, but we worked around it by adding a retry mechanism. Next week we plan to finish the dashboard UI and begin user acceptance testing. We need sign-off from the product team by Wednesday for this to stay on track."

- Stop speaking — Wispr Flow will automatically type your words into the document, cleanly punctuated

**Step 6: Use the Refine Button**
- After dictation, a small **"Refine"** button will appear near your text
- Click it and choose: `"Make this more formal and professional"`
- Watch it rewrite your dictated speech into polished business language

**Step 7: Try More Dictation Use Cases**
- Click in a new area of the document and dictate a subject line for an email:
  *Say:* "Write a follow-up email subject line for a client meeting about the product demo delayed to next Tuesday"
- Wispr Flow transcribes and can help format it appropriately

## 📅 DAY 5 — Julius AI: Data Analysis Like a Pro
**Theme:** Turn raw numbers into powerful insights in minutes

---

### 🎯 Scenario: Analysing 6 Months of Sales Data for a Small Retail Shop

You manage a stationery shop and have 6 months of daily sales data. You want to understand which products sell best, which months are slowest, and whether your weekend promotions are working.

---

### 🔧 Preparing Your Dataset

**Step 1: Create a Sample CSV File**
- Open Google Sheets or Excel
- Create a spreadsheet with these columns:
  `Date | Product | Category | Units Sold | Revenue (₹) | Day of Week | Promotion Active`

- Add approximately 30–40 rows of sample data spanning January–June 2025:

```
15-Jan-25 | Blue Pen | Stationery | 50 | 500 | Wednesday | No
15-Jan-25 | Notebook A4 | Notebooks | 20 | 1200 | Wednesday | No
18-Jan-25 | Colour Markers | Art | 15 | 1350 | Saturday | Yes
22-Jan-25 | Sticky Notes | Stationery | 40 | 800 | Wednesday | No
...
(continue with similar rows, varying by month and category)
```

- Make sure to have some weekend rows where "Promotion Active" is "Yes" and higher sales
- Save as `shop_sales_data.csv`

---

### 🔧 Julius AI — Step-by-Step Instructions

**Step 2: Open Julius AI**
- Go to **julius.ai** in your browser
- Sign in with Google or email
- The free tier allows a limited number of analyses per day — more than enough for practice

**Step 3: Upload Your Dataset**
- On the Julius home screen, click **"Upload File"**
- Select your `shop_sales_data.csv` file
- Wait for Julius to load and preview the data

**Step 4: Ask Your First Analysis Question**
- In the chat box, type:
  `"Give me a complete overview of this dataset — total revenue, best selling product, worst performing month, and average daily sales"`
- Read the response carefully — Julius will show both the analysis text and automatically generate charts

**Step 5: Drill Down on Products**
- Type: `"Which category generates the most revenue? Show me a bar chart comparing all categories"`
- A bar chart will appear — observe which category dominates

**Step 6: Weekend vs Weekday Analysis**
- Type: `"Compare average revenue on weekdays vs weekends. Also check if Promotion Active = Yes leads to higher sales"`
- This tests whether your weekend promotions are actually working

**Step 7: Monthly Trend Analysis**
- Type: `"Show me monthly revenue as a line chart from January to June. Identify which month had the steepest growth and which had a decline"`

**Step 8: Find Correlations**
- Type: `"Is there a correlation between the day of the week and units sold? Show a heatmap if possible"`

**Step 9: Forecasting**
- Type: `"Based on the trend in this data, forecast the revenue for July and August 2025"`
- Julius will project future numbers using statistical extrapolation

**Step 10: Get Actionable Business Advice**
- Type: `"Based on all this analysis, give me 5 specific recommendations to increase revenue in the next 3 months"`

**Step 11: Create a PowerPoint Report**
- Type: `"Create a PowerPoint presentation summarising all the key findings from this analysis with charts included. I want to present this in my next business review meeting"`
- Julius will generate and provide a downloadable .pptx file

**Step 12: Export a Summary**
- Type: `"Create a one-page executive summary as a downloadable PDF"`

---

### 📊 Expected Outcome by End of Day 5

- You understand how to extract business insights without knowing statistics

### 🎯 Scenario: Preparing for a Data Analyst Role at a Tech Startup

You have 2 years of experience in Excel and basic SQL. You want to apply for a "Data Analyst" role at a product startup. Today you will practise your interview with an AI coach and then find and apply for matching jobs — all using free AI tools. Have your existing resume (even a rough one) ready as a PDF or text file before you begin.

---

### Part A: Mock Interview Practice with ChatGPT Voice

**Step 1: Prepare a Brief Role Summary**
- Before opening any tool, spend 5 minutes writing down:
  - Your current job title and years of experience
  - 2–3 skills you are confident about (e.g., Excel, SQL, data visualisation)
  - The type of Data Analyst role you are targeting (e.g., product analytics, business intelligence)

**Step 2: Open ChatGPT**
- Go to **chatgpt.com** on your mobile or desktop
- Sign in or create a free account
- Start a new chat

**Step 3: Set Up the Interviewer**
- Type this system prompt to configure ChatGPT as your interviewer:

  `"You are a strict but fair interviewer at a tech startup called DataFlow. You are interviewing me for a Data Analyst position requiring 1–3 years of experience with Excel, SQL, and data storytelling. Ask me one question at a time, wait for my full answer, then give me specific feedback on what was good and what could be improved before moving to the next question. **Step 4: Switch to Voice Mode**

- Speak naturally — treat it as a real interview

  1. "Tell me about yourself and why you are interested in this role"
  2. "Walk me through how you would clean a messy dataset in Excel"

  6. "How do you handle conflicting data from two different source systems?"

**Step 6: Request a Full Evaluation**
- After 5–7 questions, switch back to text mode and type:
  `"Give me a complete evaluation of my interview performance. Score me on: communication clarity, technical accuracy, structure of answers using the STAR method, and confidence. Then give me 3 specific areas to improve before my real interview."`

**Step 7: Run a Second Round Focused on Weaknesses**

## 📅 DAY 7 — n8n + Claude Dispatch Mode: AI Automation
**Theme:** Set up an AI that works for you 24/7

---

### 🎯 Scenario A (n8n): Building an Automated Meeting Follow-Up System

Every time you have a meeting that gets logged in your Google Calendar, you want an AI to automatically send a follow-up email to all attendees with a summary of next steps. You will build this automation today using n8n's free tier.

---

### 🔧 n8n — Step-by-Step Instructions

**Step 1: Create a Free n8n Account**
- Go to **n8n.io** in your browser
- Click **"Get Started Free"**
- Sign up with your email (no credit card needed)
- You get a free cloud workspace with up to 5 active workflows

**Step 2: Access the Workflow Canvas**
- After logging in, click **"Create Workflow"**
- You will see an empty canvas — this is where you build your automation

**Step 3: Add the Trigger Node**
- Click the **"+"** icon in the centre
- Search for and select **"Google Calendar"**
- Choose trigger: **"Event Created"** — this fires whenever a new calendar event is added
- Authenticate with your Google account when prompted
- Set calendar to: `Primary Calendar`
- This trigger will fire anytime a new meeting is created

**Step 4: Add an AI Processing Node**
- Click the **"+"** after the trigger
- Search for **"OpenAI"** (free tier available with your own key, or use n8n's AI node)
- Configure it with this system message:
  `"You are a professional assistant. Given a meeting title, date, time, and attendee names, write a brief and professional follow-up email. Include: a thank you line, placeholder text for 3 action items with [Action Item] tags, and a next steps section. Keep it under 150 words."`
- In the user message, use dynamic data: `"Meeting: {{$json.summary}} | Date: {{$json.start.dateTime}} | Attendees: {{$json.attendees}}"`

**Step 5: Add a Gmail Node**
- Click **"+"** after the OpenAI node
- Search for **"Gmail"** and select **"Send Email"**
- Authenticate with your Google account
- Set:
  - **To:** `{{$json.attendees[0].email}}` (you can loop through all attendees)
  - **Subject:** `Follow-up: {{$('Google Calendar').item.json.summary}}`
  - **Body:** `{{$json.choices[0].message.content}}` (the AI-generated email)

**Step 6: Add a Delay Node (Optional)**
- Add a **"Wait"** node between the trigger and the AI node
- Set it to 30 minutes — this gives the meeting time to finish before sending the follow-up

**Step 7: Test the Workflow**
- Click **"Test Workflow"** at the top
- Manually trigger it by clicking the Google Calendar node and clicking **"Fetch Test Event"**
- Trace the data through each node to verify the email is generated correctly

**Step 8: Activate the Workflow**
- Toggle the workflow to **"Active"** (green switch in top right)
- Create a test calendar event in Google Calendar with 2 attendees (use email addresses you can access)
- Wait and verify the follow-up email arrives

## 💡 Pro Tips for Getting the Most Out of This Bootcamp

1. **Be specific in your prompts** — The more detail you give, the better the output. Never write a 5-word prompt for a complex task.
2. **Iterate, don't restart** — If the output isn't perfect, follow up with corrections rather than starting over.
3. **Combine tools** — Use NotebookLM to research → Claude to build → Julius to analyse → n8n to automate. The real power is in chaining tools.
4. **Save your prompts** — Keep a personal "prompt library" in a Google Doc of prompts that worked well for you.
5. **Free tier strategy** — Space out heavy usage across days. Most free tiers reset daily. If you hit a limit, come back the next day.
6. **Document your wins** — Screenshot every result. These become your portfolio to show in interviews or to clients.
